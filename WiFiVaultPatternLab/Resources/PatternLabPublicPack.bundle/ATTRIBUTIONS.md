# PatternLab Public Pack 3.0 — Data notices

This bundle contains only public aggregate language/place/name data, generated keyboard-adjacency patterns, and one project-owner supplied performance fixture. It contains no account records, network captures, leaked credential database, SSIDs, phone-number corpus, or person-linked identity records.

## English roots

- Source: `an-array-of-english-words` 2.0.0, derived from the Letterpress word list.
- Upstream: https://github.com/words/an-array-of-english-words
- Transformation: ASCII-only entries, 3–24 characters, normalized and truncated deterministically to 200,000 unique roots.
- License: MIT. See `Licenses/EnglishWords-MIT.txt`.

## Pinyin roots

- Source: CC-CEDICT data distributed through `cc-cedict` 1.1.1.
- Upstream: https://www.mdbg.net/chinese/dictionary?page=cedict
- Transformation: readings with 2–5 syllables were lowercased, tone numbers/spaces removed, `ü/u:` represented as `v`, ranked deterministically, deduplicated, and truncated to 50,000 roots. English definitions and Chinese entries are not included.
- License: CC BY-SA 4.0. The derivative data file remains available under the same license. See `Licenses/CC-CEDICT-BY-SA-4.0.txt`.

## Global city roots

- Source: GeoNames `cities15000`, distributed through `cities-15000-structured` 1.0.1.
- Upstream: https://download.geonames.org/export/dump/
- Transformation: ASCII city names were normalized for password-structure education, ordered by population, deduplicated, and truncated to 20,000 roots. Coordinates and other location fields are not included.
- License: CC BY 4.0. Credit: GeoNames, https://www.geonames.org/. See `Licenses/GeoNames-CC-BY-4.0.txt`.

## Common first-name roots

- Source: U.S. Social Security Administration national baby-name aggregate, 1880–2021, distributed through `us-baby-names-2` 1.0.2.
- Upstream: https://www.ssa.gov/oact/babynames/limits.html
- Transformation: aggregate occurrence counts were summed by first name; names were normalized, ranked by total frequency, deduplicated, and truncated to 20,000 roots. No person, date of birth, Social Security number, or other identity record is present.
- Notice: CC0-1.0 / U.S. government open data. See `Licenses/SSA-Names-CC0-1.0.txt`.

## Keyboard patterns

- Source: generated by the included deterministic QWERTY adjacency script.
- Transformation: common row/column patterns plus deterministic adjacent-key walks, 500 unique entries.

## Test dataset

- Source: supplied by the project owner as a performance fixture and registered as `test_dataset` exactly as requested.
- It is excluded from common-root analysis and is disabled by default.
- Before public App Store redistribution, the project owner should retain documentary proof that redistribution of this specific fixture is permitted.
